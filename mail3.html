Common Questions

How long is our session? 

A typical session is 1.5 hours. Some people want to stay longer, which we happily arrange pending availability. We don’t recommend staying for longer than 3 hours.

Other than pack boxes, is there anything else to do at Pantry Packers? 

Yes! For children, there is a coloring corner and game kiosks. For adults, we recommend leaving an extra 10-15 minutes so you can explore our on-site museum, which takes you through a long history of welfare support in Israel.

What ages are welcome at Pantry Packers?

Everyone! From toddlers to centenarians, there’s something for everyone. As we like to say, it’s “Tzedakah with the whole family.” Some kids love helping. Others enjoy the coloring corner. And our oldest volunteer has been in her 90s! 
(Children under 7 require a chaperone.)

Is it wheelchair accessible?

Yes. The entire volunteer center is fully accessible – and we’ll help accommodate any specific needs.

Are there volunteer tasks that can be done sitting down?

Yes. If you’d like a chair, simply let your guide know and we’ll bring one to your station.

Do we give our suggested $32 donation before or at the volunteer center?

You’ll be able to make your donation using one of our Tzedakah Kiosks at Pantry Packers.
